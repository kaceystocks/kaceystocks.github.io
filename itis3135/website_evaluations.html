Website Evaluations

Poorly Designed Website - craigslist

Craigslist is an online classifieds website where users can post and browse listings for jobs, housing, services, items for sale, and community events. It has been around since the mid-1990s but has not significantly updated its design.

  • Contrast: Very minimal; the site uses mostly plain text and links without clear hierarchy, making it visually unappealing.
  • Repetition: Inconsistent; while the basic layout repeats, font sizes and spacing feel outdated and uncoordinated.
  • Alignment: Poor; many sections are simply text links stacked together without strong alignment cues, giving a cluttered appearance.
  • Proximity: Weak; related elements are not always grouped together clearly, which makes navigation confusing.

Overall, Craigslist does not follow the CRAP principles well. While functional, its outdated and cluttered design makes it difficult to navigate and visually unappealing.

Well Designed Website - More Nutrition

More Nutrition is a modern website for a nutrition brand, showcasing its products with a clean layout, strong visuals, and clear calls to action. It uses professional design principles to guide the user experience.

  • Contrast: Excellent; the site uses bold colors, large headings, and high-quality images to clearly separate elements.
  • Repetition: Strong; consistent fonts, colors, and button styles reinforce branding throughout the site.
  • Alignment: Very good; grids and spacing create a professional, balanced layout thatâ€™s easy to follow.
  • Proximity: Effective; related content is grouped logically, making it easy to find information and products.

Overall, More Nutrition follows the CRAP principles very well. Its design is visually engaging, consistent, and user-friendly, making it easy for visitors to explore the brand and products.
